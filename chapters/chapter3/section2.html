3.2: From Brainstorm to Blueprint

AI Prompts to Structure Your Ideas & Outline Plans

The Problem

Your mind is buzzing with ideas – for a new service, a marketing campaign, improvements to a process, or content for a presentation.

However, these thoughts can often feel scattered, making it difficult to translate them into a coherent structure or an actionable plan.

The AI Prompt Solution

Think of AI as your organizational partner. It can help you take a jumble of brainstormed thoughts and group them into logical themes, create outlines for projects or presentations, and bring structure to your creative process.

Prompt Example 1: Structuring Brainstormed Ideas

Why it Works

This prompt allows the AI to act as an objective sorter. It can identify relationships between disparate ideas that you might miss when you're too close to the brainstorming process, helping to bring clarity and order.

Expected Outcome for You

Transform a scattered list of ideas into a structured set of themes or categories. This makes it easier to see the bigger picture, prioritize actions, and begin developing a concrete plan.

Copy & Modify This Prompt

I've brainstormed a list of ideas related to [Topic, e.g., 'improving customer onboarding']. Please help me organize them.

My raw ideas are:

[Idea 1, e.g., 'Welcome email series']
[Idea 2, e.g., 'Personalized demo call']
[Idea 3, e.g., 'Checklist for new clients']
[Idea 4, e.g., 'Video tutorials for key features']
[Idea 5, e.g., 'Dedicated onboarding specialist']
[Idea 6, e.g., 'Feedback survey after 30 days']
[Add as many ideas as you have]
Please:

Group these ideas into logical categories or themes.
For each category, provide a brief heading.
Suggest a potential order or flow if these were to be implemented.

Refining Your Structured Ideas – Thinking Laterally

Once the AI has categorized and ordered your brainstormed ideas, you can delve deeper into their strategic implications or prepare for more detailed planning.

The Goal of this Refiner

To further analyze the structured ideas for dependencies, priority, potential impact, or to generate new avenues for exploration.

Why This Refiner Works

It helps transition from simple organization to more strategic thinking and action planning.

  • Option 1 aids in prioritization and resource allocation.
  • Option 2 helps in realistic planning and sequencing.
  • Option 3 opens up new avenues for creative problem-solving based on the initial ideas.
  • Option 4 introduces a results-oriented perspective early in the planning process.

Expected Enhanced Outcome for You

A set of organized ideas that are further enriched with strategic considerations like priority, dependencies, avenues for deeper exploration, and potential success measures, making your initial brainstorm even more actionable.

Copy & Modify This Refiner Prompt

Review the categorized and structured list of ideas related to [Topic] [or paste it here]. Now, analyze this list further in ONE of the following ways:

1. Identify "Quick Wins" vs. "Strategic Initiatives": For each category or major idea, classify it as either a potential "Quick Win" (relatively easy to implement, likely to show visible results quickly) or a "Strategic Initiative" (may require more resources/time but has significant long-term impact).

2. Suggest Potential Dependencies: Review the ideas and identify any obvious dependencies between them (e.g., "Idea A must be completed before Idea B can start").

3. Generate "How Might We..." Questions: For each main category of ideas, formulate one or two "How Might We..." (HMW) questions that could be used to spark more detailed brainstorming or problem-solving related to that theme. (e.g., If a category is "Improve Communication," a HMW could be "How might we make our weekly updates more engaging for clients?").

4. Brainstorm Potential KPIs/Success Metrics: For 2-3 of the key ideas or categories, suggest one potential Key Performance Indicator (KPI) or success metric that could be used to measure its effectiveness if implemented.

Prompt Example 2: Creating a Simple Project or Presentation Outline

Why it Works

By providing the topic, audience, key message, and any constraints (like time), you give the AI the necessary parameters to create a relevant and well-structured outline. This is a great starting point that overcomes the "blank page" hurdle.

Expected Outcome for You

A clear, logical outline for your project or presentation, ready for you to flesh out with details. This saves significant planning time and ensures your content flows effectively to achieve your communication goals.

Copy & Modify This Prompt

I need to create an outline for a [Project/Presentation] on the topic of "[Your Topic, e.g., 'The Benefits of AI for Small Business Communication']".

The target audience is [e.g., 'Small business owners who are new to AI'].
The key message I want to convey is [e.g., 'AI can save significant time and improve clarity in daily business communications without being overly complex'].

I have approximately [e.g., '30 minutes for the presentation / This is a small internal project'].

Please generate a logical outline with main sections and potential sub-points for each.

For a presentation, suggest where I might include an introduction, key examples, and a call to action.

For a project, suggest phases or key deliverable areas

Refining Your Project/Presentation Outline – Thinking Laterally

After the AI drafts the initial outline, you can enrich it by considering audience engagement, resource needs, success measurement, or alternative approaches.

The Goal of this Refiner

To make the outline more robust and practical by adding layers related to delivery, execution, impact assessment, or creative flexibility.

Why This Refiner Works

It encourages a more holistic view of the project or presentation, beyond just the content itself.

  • Option 1 (Presentations) makes the delivery more dynamic and memorable.
  • Option 2 (Projects) prompts early thought about practical execution needs.
  • Option 3 instills a results-driven mindset from the planning stage.
  • Option 4 provides creative flexibility and ensures the chosen structure is the most effective.

Expected Enhanced Outcome for You

An outline that is not only well-structured but also considers aspects of effective delivery (for presentations), practical resourcing (for projects), impact measurement, and structural alternatives, leading to more successful outcomes.

Copy & Modify This Refiner Prompt

Review the drafted [project/presentation] outline for "[Your Topic]" [or paste the outline here]. Now, enhance it in ONE of the following ways:

1. Suggest Audience Engagement Techniques (for Presentations): For 2-3 key sections in the presentation outline, suggest a specific, simple audience engagement technique (e.g., "Ask a quick poll question here," "Share a brief, relatable anecdote to illustrate this point," "Pause for a 1-minute 'reflect and jot down' activity").

2. Identify Key Resource Requirements (for Projects): For each major phase or section in the project outline, list 1-2 probable key resources that would be needed (e.g., "Personnel: Graphic Designer," "Budget: Software Subscription," "Tool: Project Management Platform," "Information: Competitor Analysis Data").

3. Propose Methods for Measuring Success/Impact: Add a final section to the outline titled "Measuring Success." Suggest 2-3 ways the success of this [project/presentation] could be gauged or its impact measured (e.g., "Post-presentation survey for understanding," "Project: Number of leads generated," "Project: Reduction in X by Y%").

4. Offer an Alternative Structural Flow: Suggest one significantly different structural flow or organizational approach for the content in the outline. For example, if the current outline is chronological, suggest a problem/solution structure, or a thematic approach. Briefly explain the potential benefit of this alternative.
